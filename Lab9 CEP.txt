Q2:
Scenario A: Event planning
Constraints & Requirements

Budget: $500 total

Number of people: 20

Type of event: team-building

Must include: venue ideas, food options, activities
Prompt 1 – Generate Event Ideas

“Plan a team-building event for 20 people with a $500 budget. Give me 3–5 simple ideas, including activity, approximate cost, and what materials are needed.”

Prompt 2 – Venue Options

“Suggest affordable or free venues for a 20-person team-building event. Include options like parks, meeting rooms, community centers, or company office spaces.”

Prompt 3 – Activity Planning

“Give me 5 team-building activities that cost little or no money. Include a short description, how long each activity takes, and what materials we need.”

Prompt 4 – Budget Breakdown

“Make a simple budget plan for the event with a $500 limit. Include food, drinks, materials, and any venue costs.”

Prompt 5 – Iterative Refinement

“Here’s the idea I like most: [insert idea]. Improve the plan, suggest cheaper alternatives, and make the schedule more organized.”

Chain-of-Thought Use

Start with broad ideas

Narrow down to venue

Then activities

Then budget

Finally refine the chosen plan

scenario B:
Constraints & Requirements

You are a beginner

Using a phone camera

Goal: improve social media photos

Want learning in simple steps

Prefer tutorials, checklists, and practice tasks

Prompt 1 – Skill Assessment

“Ask me 5 questions to understand my photography level so you can give me personalized tips for social media photos.”

Prompt 2 – Basic Tutorial

“Explain the basics of photography for beginners using a phone camera. Make it simple. Use bullet points.”

Prompt 3 – Checklist

“Make a quick checklist I can use before taking a photo. Keep it short and easy to follow.”

Prompt 4 – Practice Exercises

“Give me 5 beginner-friendly practice exercises to improve my lighting, angles, and composition.”

Prompt 5 – Personalized Tips

“I mostly take pictures of [portraits/food/selfies/etc.]. Give me 10 simple tips to improve that type of photo.”

Prompt 6 – Learning Plan

“Create a 2-week practice plan to improve my phone photography. One small task per day.”

Chain-of-Thought Use

Start with skill check

Learn the basics

Do practice tasks

Get personalized advice

Build a learning plan

Scenario C:
Constraints & Requirements

Budget: limited (you choose an amount, e.g., $300)

Updating living room

Want ideas that are creative but affordable

Must include: colour ideas, furniture advice, decoration options

Prompt 1 – Describe the Room

“Ask me questions about my living room so you can give personalized improvement ideas. Ask about size, colours, lighting, and budget.”

Prompt 2 – Theme Options

“Suggest 3–4 design styles that match a low budget. Keep explanations simple and include colour palettes.”

Prompt 3 – Budget-Friendly Changes

“Give me cheap ways to improve my living room. Include ideas for lighting, furniture rearrangement, and decorations under $50.”

Prompt 4 – Compare Options

“Compare these two design ideas: [insert two options]. Tell me which one fits my room better and why.”

Prompt 5 – Shopping List

“Create a shopping list for the design style I choose. Keep everything within my budget.”

Chain-of-Thought Use

Start by gathering info

Get themes

Explore cheap upgrades

Compare choices

Finalize with a shopping list
